The Alzheimer's disease Algorithmic Diagnostic Helper

DxSter, the Alzheimer's disease Algorithmic Diagnostic Helper, is a codified algorithm that can diagnose normal cognition, MCI and Dementia which services as a valid alternative that reduces time, effort and biases associated with consensus diagnosis. Codifying the AlgDx algorithmic diagnosis project is an effort to translate the algorithm that calculates the physician and clinical diagnosis. The algorithm is designed to reduce the number of cases that need review in consensus conference. To accomplish this Dr. Meredith Wicklund from the University of Florida has been awarded a grant through the Ed and Ethel Moore Alzheimer’s Research Program in which CTS-IT has been provided funding to translate the AlgDx into Python and JavaScript software libraries.

What is Clinical Dx?

The Clinical Dx is the physician's cognitive diagnosis. After reviewing a patients clinical history, including his or her CDR rating, the Functional Activity Questionanaire, and the MMSE score and sub scores, the physician will determine a cognitive diagnosis for the patient: NCI, MCI, or Dementia. "Factors that could modify the physician’s impression about the subject’s cognitive and functional abilities were taken into consideration. These factors included the subject’s educational and cultural background, sensory (especially visual and hearing) and motor deficits, language and speech disorders, medical and psychiatric conditions and the perceived reliability of the informant." For the purposes of the algorithm the Clinical Dementia Rating Sum of Boxes score is used to represent the clinical diagnosis.1

1 Duara, R., Loewenstein, D. A., Greig, M., Acevedo, A., Potter, E., Appel, J., … Potter, H. (2010). Reliability and Validity of an Algorithm for the Diagnosis of Normal Cognition, MCI and Dementia: Implications for Multi-Center Research Studies. The American Journal of Geriatric Psychiatry : Official Journal of the American Association for Geriatric Psychiatry, 18(4), 363–370.

What is Neuropsychological Dx?

The Neuropsychological Dx is determined by a test battery given in the native language of the patient (English or Spanish). A typical battery of NACC and non-NACC tests is very large, meaning it has a higher probablity of spurious findings. Therefore the size of the diagnostic battery is limited when determining the Neuropsychological Dx. Memory function tests given include: 3-trial Fuld Object Memory Evaluation and Delayed Visual Reproduction of the Wechsler Memory Scale-R. Non-memory function tests given include: category fluency, letter fluency, Block Design-WAIS-III, Trails B, and Similarities-WAIS-R. For the purposes of the algorithm the possible options are Normal, Pre-MCI, Early-MCI, Late MCI, and Dementia to represent the neuropsychological diagnosis.1
